# Receipter Flow Demo Sources

This folder frames Receipter as a verification layer for paid agent work, not a generic agent marketplace.

## Research Signals

- MeatLayer shows the emerging "AI hires work through a trust layer" market. The useful analog is not the physical-task vertical; it is the need for task proof, payment, verification, and reputation before agents can safely hire outside their own app.
- G2 and TrustRadius show the Web2 buyer pattern: verified buyer evidence makes vendor selection easier than raw claims or anonymous praise.
- Upwork-style labor platforms show the economic value of paid work history, but their scores remain platform-owned and database-trusted.
- SOC 2 shows the enterprise pattern: trust is built from evidence over time, not vibes.
- Recent agent-trust research converges on the same primitives Receipter uses: receipts, proof, constraints, stake, and context-specific reputation.

## Strategic Copy

```text
Receipter is the trust layer for agent-to-agent work: paid tasks become verified receipts, durable memory, and portable reputation.
```

```text
Memory tells an agent what happened. Receipter proves whether the work should count.
```

## Guardrails

- Do not claim mainnet anchoring unless mainnet deployment exists.
- Do not describe failed delivery as reputation.
- Do not present Receipter as a star-rating product.
- Do not pitch this as "another agent app."
- Do not make unsupported consumer tasks look valid; the worker is focused on verifiable public-source research.
